Do's and Dont's

Do's

  • DO wash your hands for at least 20 seconds, several times a day.
  • DO consider taking extra precautions and staying out of public places if you’re over 60 years old.
  • DO practice “social distancing”: Stay 3- to 6-feet away from other people, especially if they're coughing or sneezing.
  • DO prioritize your health. Now is not the time to burn the candle at both ends, skip workouts, or ignore a healthy diet—that can weaken your immune system
  • DO seek help early if you have a fever, cough, and a hard time breathing.
  • DO cough or sneeze into the crook of your elbow or a tissue, and dispose of the tissue immediately in a covered bin.

Dont's

  • DON’T touch your eyes, nose, and mouth
  • DON’T wear a mask unless you’re sick. Masks help protect others from catching the virus, but wearing one when you’re healthy won’t do much
  • DON’T assume young people won’t get gravely ill or even die. As of March 16, 38% of all hospitalizations were of people 20-to-54 years old.
  • DON’T go to the doctor unless it’s urgent.
  • DON’T hang out with your family or pets if you suspect you have the virus.
  • DON’T hesitate to seek help immediately if someone in your home is experiencing warning signs
